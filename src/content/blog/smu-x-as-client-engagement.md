It is easy to treat a school project as a school project: hit the rubric,
hit the deadline, move on. Treating it instead like a client engagement
changes what "done" means.

## Three shifts that mattered

1. **Define the actual client problem, not the assignment prompt.** The
   brief tells you the deliverable format; it rarely tells you what the
   "client" actually needs to decide as a result.
2. **Write the recommendation before the deck is finished.** Forces early
   clarity on what you're arguing, instead of discovering the argument while
   assembling slides at 11pm.
3. **Assume someone will act on this.** Even when no one literally will, the
   discipline of writing for a decision-maker — not a grader — changes the
   quality bar.
