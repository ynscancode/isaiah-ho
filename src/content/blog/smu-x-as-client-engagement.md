It is easy to treat a school project as a school project: hit the rubric,
hit the deadline, move on. Treating it instead like a client engagement
changes what "done" means.

Three shifts that mattered. First, define the actual client problem, not the
assignment prompt. The brief tells you the deliverable format; it rarely
tells you what the "client" actually needs to decide as a result. Second,
write the recommendation before the deck is finished. Forces early clarity
on what you're arguing, instead of discovering the argument while assembling
slides at 11pm. Third, assume someone will act on this. Even when no one
literally will, the discipline of writing for a decision-maker — not a
grader — changes the quality bar.
